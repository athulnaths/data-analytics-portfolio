# HR Employee Attrition Analysis | Power BI

## Project Overview
This project analyzes employee attrition using HR data to identify key drivers of employee turnover. The dashboard helps HR and leadership teams understand retention risks and make data-driven workforce decisions.

---

## Objectives
- Measure overall employee attrition
- Identify high-risk employee segments
- Analyze factors influencing employee turnover
- Support strategic HR planning using data insights

---

## Dataset Description
- Dataset: HR-Employee-Attrition.xlsx
- Total Employees: 1,470
- Total Attributes: 35
- Key Fields:
  - Attrition
  - Department
  - JobRole
  - MonthlyIncome
  - WorkLifeBalance
  - YearsAtCompany

---

## Key KPIs
- Total Employees
- Attrition Count
- Attrition Rate (%)
- Average Monthly Income
- Average Years at Company

---

## Key Insights
- Overall attrition rate is ~16%
- Sales department shows higher attrition
- Early-tenure and low-income employees are at higher risk
- Frequent travel negatively impacts retention
- Career stagnation increases attrition probability

---

## Analytical & Business Questions

- What is the overall employee attrition rate?
- Which departments and job roles have the highest attrition?
- How does attrition vary by job level and tenure?

- Does frequent business travel increase attrition?
- How does monthly income impact employee retention?
- Are employees with lower job satisfaction more likely to leave?

- Which employee segments are at highest attrition risk?
- What retention strategies can reduce early-tenure attrition?
- How can promotions and career growth improve retention?

---

## Data Validation
- Verified total employee count using raw row count
- Validated attrition totals against categorical aggregation
- Ensured no duplicate employee records
- Used consistent filters across all visuals
